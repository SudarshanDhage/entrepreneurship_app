## 37 Legendary Pitch Deck Examples

- **Source:** Piktochart Blog
- **Author:** Aaron Lee
- **URL:** https://piktochart.com/blog/startup-pitch-decks-what-you-can-learn/
- **Date:** 2025
- **Featured:** Aaron Lee, Piktochart team
- **Referenced:** 37 successful startup pitch decks including Facebook, TikTok, Airbnb, YouTube, Dropbox, Uber, Buffer, Square, LinkedIn, Mint, Front, Mixpanel, Moz, BuzzFeed, Peloton, Tinder, Sequoia Capital, and many others
- **Key Concepts:** Pitch decks, investor presentations, fundraising, startup pitches, visual storytelling, problem-solution narrative, market opportunity, business model, financials, social proof, competitive differentiation, team slide
- **Key Insight:** Comprehensive analysis of 37 successful pitch decks that raised millions in funding. Core principles: (1) Pitch decks don't have to be formal or beautiful - prioritize clarity and effectiveness. (2) Provide impactful intro/slogan - describe business in few words, like telling a 5-year-old. (3) Keep deck short (less than 20 pages, ideally 10-15 slides). (4) Use analogies to back up points. What to include: strong intro/vision, show problems and solutions, identify market opportunities, showcase products/services clearly, digest business model, highlight financials, add social proof/case studies, differentiate from competition, show experienced management team. Key takeaways from examples: Facebook focused on solid numbers (user engagement, traffic, growth). TikTok showed virtuous cycle visualization (growth flywheel). Airbnb's intro hooks audience with simplicity. YouTube wanted to be primary outlet for video content. Dropbox kept it simple and relatable, addressing common problems with clear language. Uber was clean and minimalist, clear problem-solution flow. Buffer based deck on solid numbers from users. Square promoted management team for social proof. LinkedIn used analogies (Web 1.0 vs 2.0). Mint showed clear value proposition and exit strategy options. Front used visual storytelling. Peloton differentiated with "Who We Are? & What We Are Not?" slide. Tinder proved simplicity works - clear problem, innovative solution, solid market analysis. Avoid overcomplicating, too many slides, jargon overload, missing the hook, ignoring the problem. DocSend analyzed 200 startups who raised $360 million from their first pitch deck - patterns show clarity, simplicity, strong narrative, and solid data win.

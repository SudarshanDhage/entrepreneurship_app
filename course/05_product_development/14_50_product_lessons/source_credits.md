## 50 Short Product Lessons

- **Source:** Cutlefish Blog
- **Author:** John Cutler
- **URL:** https://cutle.fish/blog/50-product-lessons
- **Date:** 2025
- **Key Concepts:** Product development, bets, opportunities, interventions, premature convergence, missions, projects, starting together, coherence, operating models, data, measurement, chronic vs acute issues, short-term thinking, velocity, storytelling, strategy, prioritization, learning cadence, learned helplessness, feature factories, customer connection, iteration, decision-making, deadlines, forcing functions, dependencies, temporary products, vocabulary, reflection
- **Key Insight:** Comprehensive collection of 50 short, conversational product lessons covering the full spectrum of product development. Key themes include: (1) Thinking in bets - portfolios of bets of different sizes and durations, communicating bets to new team members, avoiding one-size-fits-all approaches. (2) Opportunities vs interventions - framing work as opportunities and temporary interventions rather than permanent problems/solutions, enables incremental value capture. (3) Premature convergence - danger of converging on solutions too early, intuition drives early convergence, right time is "a little later than comfortable," look for period of messiness. (4) Missions vs projects - missions are open-ended, nested, have stopping functions vs predetermined definition of done, longer missions suggest value. (5) Starting together - limit planning inventory, get whole team experiencing problem for first time together (haunted house analogy), clear calendars for kickoff. (6) Coherence and messy middle - difficulty connecting low-level work to larger bets, need to keep reiterating bets in 1-3 quarter range, coherence â‰  certainty, manufacturing certainty limits coherence. (7) Multiple operating models - need different approaches for different types of bets, avoid one-size-fits-all process, higher-performing teams have stable patterns while absorbing unique work. (8) Data as trust proxy - data won't settle all disagreements, effective teams have more uncertainty, adopt learning stance, measurement is catalyst for learning not pass/fail. (9) Chronic vs acute issues - difference is self-repair ability, successful companies prevent chronic issues, immune system approach, variety in how achieved. (10) Short-term thinking - balancing act, be stubborn about long-term, compress activity durations, sense early indicators. (11) Time to get good - 12-18 months for full onboarding, requires repetition and practice, allow failure, psychological safety. (12) Play less Tetris - PMs loading up teams like Tetris, keeps people busy but prevents experimentation, over-constrains plans. (13) Moving fast and slow - bias for frequent integration + longer-term thinking, harmonize learning and shipping. (14) Storytelling - high-performing teams tell coherent stories with depth, discuss assumptions/learnings/surprises, practice reflection muscle. (15) Strategy and beliefs - "Which wave are you riding?", map core beliefs about industry direction, position relative to legacy/disruptors/competitors. (16) Prioritizing opportunities - value/effort framework is too simplistic, prioritize by opportunity size, resist premature breakdown of solutions. (17) Learning cadence - differentiate from shipping cadence, ask about Aha moments, learning velocity reveals team effectiveness, importance of acting on learning. (18) Learned helplessness - teams adapt to overly prescriptive PMs, engineers check out after being burned, need psychological safety. (19) Data snacking vs integrated - cherry-picking vs weaving data into all facets, consistent language around bets, common thread across reviews. (20) Functional feature factories - middle category (4-7 impact), chugging along but no 10s, lack step changes. (21) Teams closer to customers - "How long to get customer on phone?", remove proxies, connect problem solvers directly with humans, behavioral data + qualitative in tandem. (22) One pagers - flexible formats, checklists not requirements, don't force estimates for opportunity-focused bets. (23) Leaving time to iterate - teams scared without iteration time, frame missions with stopping functions, balance flexibility with confidence. (24) PMs creating environment - "best decisions happen reasonably quickly", not sole decider, create context for independent decisions, balance speed/quality. (25) Two priority levels - extremely valuable + experimentation-friendly OR reasonably valuable + guaranteed small, avoid medium-value trap. (26) Artificial deadlines - real deadline = value drops precipitously if missed, admit artificial deadlines, use cost of delay instead. (27) Forcing functions - healthy functions snap out of automatic thought (sprints, time boxes, customer exposure), definition from HCI glossary. (28) Measurement to inform decisions - measure what reduces uncertainty for decisions you need to make, not what competitors measure or for pass/fail. (29) Reflection is what counts - create projections at kickoff, revisit periodically, regular decision reviews, avoid revisionist history. (30) Shared understanding/vocabulary - always harder than you think, words like sprint/vision/MVP misunderstood, debate is what matters not single definition. (31) Products/features temporary - delivery mechanisms are temporary, knowledge of value exchange persists, teams around value streams not technology.

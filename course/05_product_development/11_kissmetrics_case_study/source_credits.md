## My Billion Dollar Mistake

- **Source:** Product Habits
- **Author:** Hiten Shah (Co-founder of KISSmetrics)
- **URL:** https://producthabits.com/my-billion-dollar-mistake/
- **Date:** 2018
- **Key Concepts:** Product prioritization, focus, process, execution speed, competitor analysis, drive-by management, Hiten Bombs, product-market fit, customer research, team alignment
- **Key Insight:** Comprehensive case study of how KISSmetrics, despite having a 3-year head start on the analytics market and achieving product-market fit, lost their billion-dollar opportunity. Early failures: Built products for Facebook Application Developers who couldn't pay, then built business intelligence tool requiring too much customization. Pivot that worked: Talked to Google Analytics users, identified online marketers as target market (willing to pay, needed conversion data), built MVP addressing their pain points (funnel reports, real-time debugging, user-level data). Success: Hundreds of signups per week, word-of-mouth, industry standard features, 3-year market lead. The "Hiten Bomb" problem: Daily/weekly random ideas dropped on team without framework or filter - new features, competitor-inspired ideas, investor/advisor suggestions, single customer requests. Impact: Team rushed to execute, lost confidence in leadership, stopped listening, needed internal memo to explain behavior. Root cause: No process to prioritize what to work on and why, everyone doing whatever they thought was right. What great leaders do: Consistent direction, filter inputs, remove distractions, focus resources, align team on priorities. Competitor failures: Ignored Mixpanel (mobile focus, freemium strategy, site badges, $77M raised vs KISSmetrics $25M), missed "why" behind real-time debugger (eye candy for sales demos), ignored Heap (simple API, 6,000 customers, $40M raised). Key lesson: Speed matters - can't capture market once, must do it repeatedly faster than competitors. Lost magic: Forgot process that built initial success, went back to bad habits, stalled for 3 years allowing competitors to catch up. Solution: Create product processes that produce repeatable wins based on continuous innovation, focus, planning, unbiased research, goal-focused development. Key mistakes: Building for customers who couldn't pay, requiring too much customization, making educated guesses instead of researched conclusions, no prioritization framework, drive-by management, ignoring competitors instead of learning from them, losing execution speed as team grew, forgetting successful process.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Use this for development, not just filtering.** In early stages, good ideas are very easy to kill. Be careful about using this as a filter.

2. **Product questions focus on:** Who are the users? What's their dissatisfaction? What are you building? Does it dominate on metrics that matter?

3. **Growth questions focus on:** Market size, early adopters willing to tolerate bugs, first 10 customers, playbook for growth, path to cheap capital.

4. **Strategy questions focus on:** Why now? Long-term vision (25 years), credible advances, and moats.

5. **Meaning questions focus on:** Impact on the world, personal commitment, and willingness to face failure.

6. **The character limits force clarity.** Each question has a character limit that forces you to be clear and concise about what matters.
